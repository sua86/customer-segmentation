01 · Business problem

One message,
sent to everyone.

The marketing team was treating 400,000 customers identically — same campaigns, same offers, same timing. Churn was rising and engagement was flat. The ask: find out who these customers actually are.

No customer differentiation

All marketing campaigns broadcast identically. High-value customers received the same messaging as dormant ones — wasting budget and reducing relevance.

Rising churn, unclear cause

At-risk customers couldn't be identified until after they churned. No early-warning signal existed to trigger intervention campaigns.

Data existed, insight didn't

CRM, transaction history, and engagement data were all collected — but never connected into a coherent view of customer behavior.

Leadership flying blind

No dashboard existed to monitor customer health by segment. Decisions were made on gut feel rather than observable patterns in the data.

02 · The four segments

K=4 revealed a
clear behavioural map.

After testing k=2 through k=8 using the elbow method and silhouette scores, k=4 produced the cleanest within-cluster separation and the most actionable business groupings.

Segment A

Champions

18%

of customer base

  • Highest purchase frequency
  • Top 30% spend per transaction
  • Engaged with 4+ channels
  • Low churn probability (<5%)
  • Highest NPS score

→ Loyalty program & early access

Segment B

Potential Loyalists

31%

of customer base

  • Moderate frequency, rising trend
  • Mid-range spend
  • Active on 2–3 channels
  • Recent acquisition cohort
  • High upsell potential

→ Nurture campaigns & bundles

Segment C

At-Risk

29%

of customer base

  • Declining engagement over 90d
  • Low recent transaction count
  • Previously moderate spenders
  • High churn probability (62%)
  • Price-sensitive behaviour

→ Win-back offers & surveys

Segment D

Dormant

22%

of customer base

  • No activity in 180+ days
  • Low historical spend
  • Single-channel interaction
  • Acquired via discount promo
  • Low reactivation rate

→ Low-cost reactivation or suppress

04 · Methodology

How it was built.

01

Data extraction

Pulled 18-month CRM transaction history via SQL. Joined with engagement logs from Elasticsearch.

SQL + Logstash

02

Feature engineering

Built RFM features: Recency, Frequency, Monetary value. Added engagement score and channel diversity.

pandas · numpy

03

Pre-processing

StandardScaler normalisation. Outlier capping at 99th percentile. PCA for visualisation (2D reduction).

sklearn.preprocessing

04

Model selection

Tested k=2 to k=8. Elbow method + silhouette score both indicated k=4 as optimal.

KMeans · silhouette_score

05

Profiling & naming

Computed cluster centroids. Named segments using domain knowledge and validated with marketing team.

groupby · describe()

06

BI dashboard

Exported segment labels to data warehouse. Built Power BI executive dashboard with DAX measures.

Power BI · DAX
